### Key Aspects of the Theory:  
1. **Humoral Imbalance as a Cause of Disease**:

For example, excessive humors lead to "incongruous inundation" (*incongruam inundationem*), disrupting physiological equilibrium and causing symptoms like fever, pain, and digestive disturbances [SOURCE 7].

   - The texts emphasize the interaction between humors and bodily fluids, such as the role of "bad humors" in causing putrefaction and dryness, which in turn trigger thirst and the need for moistening [SOURCE 6].  

3. **Therapeutic Implications**:
