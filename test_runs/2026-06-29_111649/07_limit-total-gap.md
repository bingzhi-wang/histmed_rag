The retrieved passages from ancient Chinese, medieval European, and Greek medical traditions do not mention **bacterial infection** or **antibiotics**. None of the texts explicitly reference microorganisms, pathogens, or antimicrobial agents. The discussions focus instead on broader concepts such as seasonal influences on disease (e.g., "病在心，愈在長夏" [SOURCE 1]), humoral imbalances (e.g., "火司于地，寒反勝之" [SOURCE 2]), prognostic indicators like urine and sweat (e.g., "τὸ δὲ οὖρον ἄριστόν ἐστιν" [SOURCE 10]), and symbolic or empirical remedies (e.g., "the heat and bitterness of the eel's bile weakens the lice" [SOURCE 6]).  

The absence of terminology related to bacterial infection or antibiotics in all sources indicates that these concepts were not part of the medical frameworks described in the passages. The texts do not address microbial etiology or chemically derived treatments for infections.
